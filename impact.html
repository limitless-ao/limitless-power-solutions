Impact framework

Power that creates more than electricity.

Every system we deploy is measured against three impact lenses — environmental, economic, and social. This is the framework we use to design, report and improve.

Environmental

Direct displacement of diesel generators reduces CO₂, NOx and particulate emissions. Each kWh from solar is a kWh not burned in fuel.

  • · kWh generated from solar
  • · Litres of diesel displaced
  • · Tonnes of CO₂ avoided

Economic

Lower fuel bills, fewer outage-related losses, and predictable monthly energy costs. For communities, more hours of productive work.

  • · Naira saved per month
  • · Downtime hours avoided
  • · Payback period

Social

Local jobs in installation and maintenance; better lighting for schools and clinics; community ownership of energy infrastructure.

  • · Technicians trained
  • · Households connected
  • · Public facilities powered

End-of-life circularity

Designed to be unmade.

Our R&D program includes a circularity plan: every component should have a defined disposal, recycling or repurposing pathway at end of life.

Reuse

Battery cells with degraded but usable capacity routed to lower-stress applications.

Recycle

Partnerships with certified recyclers for panels, electronics and metals.

Repurpose

Functional sub-assemblies redeployed to lower-spec community installations.
