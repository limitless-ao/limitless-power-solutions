R&D Program

Integrated Renewable Energy System.

A scalable hybrid AC/DC platform designed to directly replace fossil-fuel generators — safe, flexible, affordable and low-carbon — across commercial, domestic, public-sector and humanitarian use cases.

Executive summary

Replacing diesel, by design.

Diesel generators are everywhere in Nigeria — and everywhere expensive, dirty and unreliable. Our research program aims to develop and deploy a single integrated renewable system that can step into the role of a diesel generator without compromise.

The system uses both AC and DC renewable generation, integrates energy storage, can charge from the grid as a backup, and is designed to be scaled, repaired and (eventually) recycled through local supply chains.

Aligned with

ZE-Gen Accelerator (Zero-Emission Generator) and similar international climate-innovation programs replacing fossil-fuel generators with renewable alternatives.

Program objectives

Nine engineering principles.

Integrated system

Seamlessly combine multiple renewable technologies to replace one or more fossil-fuel generators.

Safety & flexibility

Engineered for safe operation across diverse applications and user scenarios.

Affordability & reliability

Cost-effective and dependable, accessible to a wide range of users.

Multi-sector applicability

Commercial, domestic, public-sector and humanitarian use cases.

Hybrid charging

AC + DC renewable generation, with grid-charge as a backup energy source.

Energy storage

Stores excess renewable energy for use during low-generation periods.

Scalability

Modular capacity to meet specific energy needs across user types.

Local supply chains

Manufacturing, installation and maintenance through local partners and labour.

End-of-life circularity

Sustainable disposal, recycling and repurposing of components at end of lifecycle.

Methodology

Six-phase research methodology.

1

Needs assessment

Detailed assessment in target areas across multiple sectors and use cases.

2

R&D

Collaboration with experts in renewable energy fields to design the system.

3

Pilot programs

Implement across multiple sectors to assess performance and gather user feedback.

4

Local partnerships

Strong partnerships with local businesses, government bodies and NGOs.

5

Training & capacity building

Programs for local technicians to install, operate and maintain the system.

6

Continuous improvement

Iterative refinement based on pilot feedback to enhance design and function.

Expected outcomes

What success looks like.

01

Reduced diesel reliance

Direct displacement of fossil-fuel generators in target sectors.

02

Improved energy access

Reliable supply for users currently dependent on intermittent grids.

03

Economic development

Local supply-chain engagement and skilled-job creation.

04

Lower carbon emissions

Quantifiable CO₂ reduction through renewable substitution.
